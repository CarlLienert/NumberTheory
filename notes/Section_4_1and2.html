    Subsection Problem 17

    Keep in mind that I did this problem on paper first and am presenting it in a "cleaned up" form.
    ðŸ”—
    Since \(b \equiv d \pmod{n}\text{,}\) \(n|b-d\) and so
    \begin{equation*} d=b-nk \end{equation*}
    for some integer \(k.\) Since \(ab \equiv cd \pmod{n}\text{,}\)
    \begin{align*} \amp n|ab-cd \\ \amp n| ab-c(b-nk)\\ \amp n|ab-cb+cnk. \end{align*}
    Since \(n|cnk\) we have
    \begin{align*} \amp n|ab-cb\\ \amp n|b(a-c) \end{align*}
    Finally, since \(\text{gcd}(n,b)=1\) we have
    \begin{equation*} n|a-c \end{equation*}
    by Euclidâ€™s lemma. That is, \(a\equiv c \pmod{n}.\)

    Subsection Problem 18

    We have:
    \begin{align*} \amp n_1|a-b \\ \amp n_2|a-c. \end{align*}
    Let \(\text{gcd}(n_1,n_2)=n\text{,}\) then
    \begin{align} \amp n|a-b\tag{2}\\ \amp n|a-c\tag{3} \end{align}
    So,
    \begin{align*} \amp n|(a-b)-(a-c)\\ \amp n|c-b . \end{align*}
    That is, \(c\equiv b \pmod{n}.\)
