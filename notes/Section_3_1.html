    Subsection Problem 15

    If \(a\) is a square, then \(a=k^2\text{.}\) Write the canonical form of \(k\text{:}\)
    \begin{equation*} k=p_1^{e_1}\cdot p_t^{e_t}. \end{equation*}
    Then
    \begin{equation*} a=k^2=p_1^{2e_1}\cdot p_t^{2e_t}. \end{equation*}
    Conversely if
    \begin{equation*} a=p_1^{2f_1}\cdots p_t^{2f_t} \end{equation*}
    then \(a\) is a square:
    \begin{equation*} a=(p_1^{f_1}\cdots p_t^{f_t})^2. \end{equation*}
