    Subsection Question 3

    For all integers \(n>1\text{,}\) \(n!\) is even. So \(n!+1\) is odd and all its prime divisors must also be odd. Let \(p\) be any prime divisor of \(n!+1.\) Suppose \(p \leq n.\) Then \(p|n!\) and so \(p|(n!+1)-(n!)\text{.}\) That is, \(p|1\) which is impossible.
