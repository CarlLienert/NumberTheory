    Subsection Question 3

    By the division algorithm, any integer, \(n\text{,}\) is of the form \(3k\text{,}\) \(3k+1\text{,}\) or \(3k+2\text{.}\) Square each of these cases:
    \begin{align*} (3k)^2 \amp =9k^2=3(3k^2) \\ (3k+1)^2 \amp = 9k^2+6k+1=3(3k^2+2k)+1 \\ (3k+2)^2 \amp = 9k^2+12k+4=3(3k^2+4k+1)+1. \end{align*}
    So the square of any integer is of the form \(3j\) or \(3j+1.\)

    Subsection Question 9

    Let \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\) and write \(n=7q+r\) where \(0 \leq r < 7\text{.}\) Consider the squares in each of the cases: (\(k\) represents some integer below. For example \(k\) would be \(7q^2\) in the first line.)
    \begin{align*} (7q)^2 \amp= 7(7q^2)\\ (7q+1)^2 \amp = 7k+1\\ (7q+2)^2 \amp =7k+4\\ (7q+3)^2 \amp =7k+2\\ (7q+4)^2 \amp =7k+2\\ (7q+5)^2 \amp = 7k+4 \\ (7q+6)^2 \amp =7k+1. \end{align*}
    And, similarly, the cubes:
    \begin{align*} (7q)^3 \amp= 7(49q^3)\\ (7q+1)^3 \amp = 7k+1\\ (7q+2)^3 \amp =7k+1\\ (7q+3)^3 \amp =7k+6\\ (7q+4)^3 \amp =7k+1\\ (7q+5)^3 \amp = 7k+6 \\ (7q+6)^3 \amp =7k+6. \end{align*}
    So, squares of integers are of the form \(7k\text{,}\) \(7k+1\text{,}\) \(7k+2\text{,}\) or \(7k+4\text{.}\) Cubes are of the form \(7k\text{,}\) \(7k+1\text{,}\) or \(7k+6\text{.}\) If an integer is both a square and a cube it must be of the form \(7k\) or \(7k+1\text{.}\)
