    Subsection Problem 11

    If \(n\) is odd, then \(n=16m+r\) where \(r\) is 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, or 15. Then
    \begin{align*} n^4+4n^2+11 \amp = (16m+r)^4+4(16m+r)^2+11\\ \amp = 16^4m^4+4\cdot16^3m^3r+6\cdot16^2m^2r^2+4\cdot16mr^3+r^4+4(16^2m^2+2\cdot16mr+r^2)+11\\ \amp = 16(\text{stuff})+ r^4+4r^2+11. \end{align*}
    It remains to check that \(r^4+4r^2+11\) is of the form \(16k\) for each of the possible values of \(r\text{.}\) (You should do this.)
